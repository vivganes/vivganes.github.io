After the [first two days](http://vivekganesan.com/blog/2015/03/29/compendium-of-agile-india-2015-track-1/) of Agile India 2015 - Scaling Agile Adoption track, it was finally the time to learn about Agile Life Cycle.  This blog post aims to create a compendium of my learnings from the Agile Life Cycle track of the conference.

Day - 3
-------
The keynote of [Dave Snowden](http://confengine.com/user/dave-snowden), titled [Embrace Complexity, Scale Agility](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4277-info) frankly went over my head!  The concepts were too complex to catch, fitting the title.  To date, I am trying to stare at his presentation and to make sense of a lot of concepts.  This served as a reminder of how long the way forward is, for me at least.

[Anand Bagmar](http://confengine.com/user/anand-bagmar)'s talk titled '[Enabling Continuous Delivery in Enterprises with Testing](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4281-info)' presented a very HUGE problem of scale and how it was solved.  This case study by Thoughtworks explains how a triangular pyramid of testing became a pentagonal pyramid when problems and situations become complex.

[Arvi Krishnaswamy](http://confengine.com/user/arvi-krishnaswamy) talked about [Lessons Learnt from an MVP](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4284-info).  The [blog post](http://arg0s.in/what-we-learnt-from-a-failed-mvp.html) he provided in the conference system is a very detailed explanation of what he did.

![Naresh Jain](https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-Sv_VlJtJmVw/VRlFYLwI1OI/AAAAAAAAFkk/agGmg0uVB9Q/w865-h577-no/REA70214.JPG)

User story slicing has consistently been a problem, which was difficult to solve.  [Naresh Jain](http://confengine.com/user/naresh-jain) addressed this concern by giving pointers on [how to effectively slice user stories](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4289-info), as a session.

Day - 4
-------
For a person like me, the topic of Behavior Driven Development was very new.  The last day's [keynote](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4304-info) was by the person ([Aslak Hellesøy](http://confengine.com/user/aslak-hellesy)) behind Cucumber.  After reading the Wikipedia's BDD page, the slides of the talk make sense to me now.

[Sophie Freiermuth](http://confengine.com/user/sophie-freiermuth) presented a talk of [Agile from the point of view of a UX person](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4308-info).  It was very informative to view the world from the eyes of a UX engineer.

![Sophie](https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-qw9yQDQlCBo/VRrBEBnYgbI/AAAAAAAAF7A/EwE5sEllIOw/w868-h577-no/DSC_1074.JPG)

I came to learn about a tool called Chef, which can be used to test infrastructure code in Test Driven Development.  Thanks to [Sreedevi](http://confengine.com/user/sreedevi-vedula)'s [session](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4313-info).

Then, it was soon time for my (Yes, my!) own (Yes, my own!) [session](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4317-info).  Another blog post is on the way about how it went.

Soon after my session was over, it was very interesting to listen to [Jeff Lopez](http://confengine.com/user/jeff-lopez-stuit), when he talked about [how Exorcist was a lean planning master](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4320-info) :)

[Ankur](http://confengine.com/user/ankur-sambhar)'s session scandalously promoted [promiscuity](http://confengine.com/agile-india-2015/schedule#session-4323-info).. in pair programming :)  The talk on his experience with the pair programming was very informative and inspiring.

After being continuously overwhelmed for four days, the conference came to an end and I travelled back to Hyderabad with lots of learnings in my heart.  Yes, the near future would be music to my organization.  I am sure of that!
